# Ametyst Market Overview

The agentic economy is creating an entirely new category of financial flows — machine-to-service transactions that existing payment infrastructure cannot support. Ametyst is built to capture these flows at the infrastructure level.

## Market Thesis

Cloud computing expanded SaaS by lowering distribution and deployment costs. AI agents introduce a second-order expansion: they transform services into software-executable units, expanding the software market from "software sold to humans" to "services executed by software." This shifts monetization from subscriptions to continuous, execution-based spending — and Ametyst positions itself as the banking infrastructure layer capturing those new transactional flows.

## Why Now — Converging Macro Trends

### 1. Agents Execute the Majority of Digital Interactions
Humans initiate intent, agents execute end-to-end. Current payment rails assume human checkout moments; agents need continuous money access during execution.

### 2. Agents Operate on Tasks, Not Time
Traditional subscription/checkout models are structurally inefficient for discrete-task execution. Banking must adapt from billing events to execution flows with granular, real-time spending.

### 3. Natural Language as the New Programming Interface
Every user can instruct agents directly. Agents dynamically generate UIs, accelerating API-first products sold in third-party frontends.

### 4. The "Fast Fashion" of SaaS and the API-First Economy
SaaS frontends become disposable; differentiation shifts to infrastructure and execution layers. Every company becomes an API provider. Value accrues to platforms enabling discovery, execution, and monetization across agentic systems.

## The Problem Gap

The current state of the art reveals a clear market gap:
- **Agentic commerce exists for goods** (e.g., Stripe's Agentic Commerce Protocol — checkout-based, human-approved purchases of physical goods)
- **Agentic payments do not exist for services** — no infrastructure for runtime, autonomous, usage-based payments by software agents
- Checkout-centric systems do not translate to runtime execution
- Human-centric financial UX does not scale to software agents
- Card-based systems are not designed for high-frequency micro-payments with programmatic authorization

## Market Focus — Why Europe

Ametyst is deliberately built in Europe, for Europe. This is a long-term strategic positioning choice, not a temporary geographic focus.

### A. Clear Market Gap
Europe currently lacks established, scaled players providing banking and payment infrastructure for AI agents built natively on stablecoins and blockchain-based execution accounts. The US fintech ecosystem is already more integrated with stablecoins, making Europe's infrastructure vacuum more pronounced.

### B. First-Mover Dynamics
Financial infrastructure markets are structurally winner-takes-most. Being first in Europe allows Ametyst to:
- Capture early volumes and become the default execution layer
- Lock in integrations early with European SaaS platforms
- Accumulate regulatory, operational, and behavioral data advantages

### C. European Compliance as a Long-Term Moat
Europe is converging toward a regulatory framework structurally different from the US in:
- Custody and delegation of funds
- Privacy and data protection (GDPR)
- Accountability of automated decision-making
- Use of AI in regulated environments (AI Act)
- Digital asset regulation (MiCA)

Ametyst designs compliance in from day one rather than retrofitting it — creating a structural moat that US-first competitors will struggle to replicate quickly.

## Target Segments

### Customers and demand side — Agent Owners
Individuals and teams deploying AI agents that need autonomous access to paid digital services during execution. Pain points: manual integration overhead, subscription nightmare, degraded agent work quality when agents cannot procure services autonomously.

### Partners and Supply Side — SaaS / API Providers
Digital platforms that want to monetize backend usage by agents on a pay-per-use basis. Pain points: cannot effectively monetize agent consumption, no viable runtime pay-per-use, forced to build walled-garden wallets or accept custom non-scalable integrations.

### Strategic Partners (Distribution Channels)
- **Billing Infrastructure & Merchant of Record platforms** (Polar, useautumn, Paid): Track consumption and manage pricing for usage-based AI products. Sit on traditional payment rails and need new infrastructure for agent monetization. Serve as both infrastructure complements and distribution channels to hundreds of SaaS platforms.
- **API Marketplaces** (RapidAPI, Apify, APILayer): Aggregate services for developers. As agents replace developers in service discovery, these evolve into agent-facing distribution layers. Integrating Ametyst enables native agentic payments for API providers.
